How Technology Costs Are Shared
Technology uses three methods to allocate costs to departments — here is what each means for your team.
Method 1 · Headcount-Based Showback
Your share of shared infrastructure
Shared technology platforms — such as the corporate network, end-user computing, and enterprise software — are allocated to each department based on how many people are in your team relative to the total headcount at BCI. A larger team receives a proportionally larger share.

This is a showback, not a hard charge. It is provided for awareness and cost transparency.

Method 2 · Consumption-Based Showback
Cloud & usage costs by what you use
Cloud computing, storage, and certain on-demand services are allocated based on actual measured consumption — the more your team uses, the more you see shown back. Usage data is collected from Azure and on-premises monitoring tools.

Some of these transition to full chargeback after a one-year showback period once the model is validated.

Method 3 · Direct Chargeback
Technology built or maintained for your LOB
Systems and services that exist specifically for one line of business — such as bespoke applications, dedicated infrastructure, or LOB-specific licences — are charged directly to that department. This is a hard cost recovery, not a showback.

You are only charged for what is yours. These amounts are agreed with your department each budget cycle.

About this report
This report reflects Technology's operating costs for FY2026 and how they are shared across BCI's lines of business. Figures are sourced from the OC Data Refresh workbook maintained by the Technology Finance team and are updated each period.

Cost allocations follow the Technology Cost Management framework approved in April 2025. Headcount-based allocations use the headcount figures as of the start of the fiscal year. Consumption-based figures reflect metered usage through 31 March 2026. Questions about your department's allocation should be directed to the Technology Finance team.

🎨 Design Rationale — What a stakeholder/viewer sees vs the admin view

  • Tabs removed: "Upload (Test)" and "Admin" are gone entirely. The viewer tabs are Overview, By Department, By Cost Type, and Technology Detail — all read-only. Removed Data Quality tab — that is an internal operational concern, not a stakeholder concern.
  • Language changed throughout: No "line items", "cost model", "showback type", "PID codes", "GL codes", or "materiality threshold". Replaced with plain English — "how many people are in your team", "what you use", "built specifically for your LOB". Stakeholders are not data analysts. Removed Item counts (37 items, 54 items) from the approach tiles in the hero.
  • "Finance Department" personalised card: The most valuable thing a viewer sees is their own department's number and why they're being charged. This card surfaces it immediately with a breakdown by method and a "what drives your bill" section using plain-English category names (End-User Computing, Cloud Infrastructure, Enterprise Licences) not GL codes. New
  • Department comparison is now a full table: All 12 departments are visible so a viewer can immediately see where they rank. "← You" label highlights their row. Technology is shown separately at the top labelled as "absorbed" so stakeholders understand it is not shared.
  • The 3 cost methods are explained in plain English: A department head should not need to ask "what does showback mean?" The method cards use paragraph-length explanations in plain language, including "this is a showback, not a hard charge" — which is the single most important thing to communicate to avoid confusion. Removed "Approach 4 · Direct / No Showback" — viewers don't need to know that $3.9M sits unallocated. That is an internal operational fact.
  • Period comparison row replaces the donut chart: The donut communicates cost split, which matters to an admin. A stakeholder wants to know: is my bill going up? The four-period comparison (FY2025 actual → FY2026 actual → FY2027 forecast → FY2028 budget) answers the trend question directly and invites them to click into future years. Removed Donut chart — no viewer needs to know showback split percentages.
  • Methodology note at the bottom: Stakeholders frequently ask "where does this data come from?" and "who do I call if I disagree?" A methodology footer answers both without cluttering the dashboard. New
  • No action items, no flags, no thresholds: The 111 data quality flags and 64 materiality items are completely hidden. These are internal Technology team concerns. Showing them to a Finance VP or LOB head would create confusion and distrust ("why am I looking at broken data?"). They belong only in the admin view. Removed
