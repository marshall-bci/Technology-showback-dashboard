🎨 Design Rationale — What I changed and why

  • ① Hero banner (dark, full-width): The most important question any finance stakeholder asks is "are we on track vs budget?" The current design doesn't answer this until you mentally subtract two KPI cards. The hero shows Total vs Budget with a variance pill and a progress bar at a glance.
  • ② Shown-back gauge: "74% of cost is actively shown back" is a more meaningful executive summary than four separate tile amounts. The ring shows the split at a glance; the legend provides the detail.
  • ③ Approach tiles with numbered labels: Tying each tile to a PPT slide number ("Approach 1 · Chargeback") directly connects the dashboard to the strategy document. The proportional bar inside each tile gives visual weight relative to the total — so a stakeholder instantly sees headcount showback dominates.
  • ④ Table-based breakdown instead of donut chart: The existing donut with 6 segments is hard to read at small sizes. A ranked table with inline bars communicates the same data faster, is easier to scan, and handles many segments gracefully.
  • ⑤ Department list instead of bar chart: The existing horizontal bar chart requires reading axis labels. A ranked list with proportional bars and explicit dollar + percentage values is faster to read and immediately shows rank. Technology is highlighted in cyan to distinguish it as the cost absorber vs the receiving LOBs.
  • ⑥ KPI strip with trend indicators: Every KPI now has a trend indicator (favourable / unfavourable / neutral) so the reader knows whether the number is good or bad without needing context. The $25K threshold and flag rate have visual warning states.
  • ⑦ Action items strip: The current design buries actionable problems in the Data Quality tab. Surfacing the top 3 on the Overview means a user opening the dashboard for the first time knows immediately what needs their attention — without having to explore every tab.
  • Narrative flow: The page reads top-to-bottom as a story: "Here is how much (hero) → here is what the strategy says (approach tiles) → here is how it breaks down (table + depts) → here is what the numbers mean (KPIs) → here is what needs your attention (action items)." Every section earns its place by advancing that story.
